DIET


How your diet can affect stress levels, for better or worse

Now, at a time when you may feel you have little control, one thing you can get a grip on is your diet choices. And interestingly, these can make or break your Stress Levels. A poor diet can bring greater reactivity toward stress.11 Emotional eating and reaching for high-fat, high-sugar foods can provide a temporary sense of relief that adds to your long-term Stress Refined carbs, like cookies and potato chips, can cause a spike in blood sugar. When your blood sugar crashes, you might experience more stress and anxiety. Consuming a healthy diet can help you combat stress over the long haul. Foods like eggs, avocado, and walnuts support mood regulation and energy balance.

FOODS TO EAT WHEN YOU ARE STRESSED:

DARK CHOCOLATE

This treat could help reduce stress in two ways, via its chemical impact and its emotional impact. Dark chocolate is also rich in antioxidants, can also lower levels of stress hormones in the body, according to a study. Just be sure to avoid excess calories in your diet by not overindulging in chocolate. Make sure ,no more than 1 ounce of dark chocolate per day.

BANANAS

The yellow, potassium rich fruit contains the mood-boosting chemical dopamine, along with magnesium, levels of which plummet during stressful times. Bananas are furthermore a rich source of certain B vitamins, such as Vitamin B6 which helps the nervous system run correctly, and can decrease stress and fatigue

MILK

Milk and other dairy with calcium and added VITAMIN D can help muscles relax and stabilize mood. calcium might not only strengthen your skeleton it could also brighten your mood.

NUTS

Nuts are a boon to your health in more ways than one. They’re satiating, meaning they can help ward off unhealthy cravings. Also they can help in lowering your blood pressure, according to a study. Their B VITAMINS have been shown to help lower stress levels.

WATER

The importance of hydration during stressful times is well-documented. Even mild dehydration can increase Cortisol Levels which contributes to increased stress. While drinking enough water will not make your stressors vanish, taking this step will help set your body up to handle stress when it does occur.

GREEN TEA

Green Tea contains an amino acid, which is receiving increasing scrutiny due to its potential effects on mood disorders. Theanine has anti-anxiety and calming effects and may increase the production of serotonin and dopamine.

LET THE MUSIC TAKE YOU AWAY



You can energize your body or relax it with the right music. You may have heard that classical music brings many benefits, but honestly the secret is any type of music that you enjoy can bring a benefit, you just need to find your type. Music with a fast tempo really can energize your physiology, and music with a calm tempo can relax your body and mind.﻿ If you find lyrics that speak to you and the mood you’re currently in (or would like to be in), you then have a great low-effort way to relieve stress while you eat—or while you walk or drive to lunch. (If you’re self-conscious about sharing your tunes, earbuds are always nice to have.)

Science Behind the Wonders of Music

When we listen to music, blood flows out of a region in the brain, contracting the muscles of that region. In the interim, there is a state of reduced pressure and progressive relaxation in that region. The repeated process causes blood to flow from the UEF(Universal Energy Field) to the HEF(Human Energy Field) transmitted by different tones of Ragas affecting the CNS(Central Nervous System).

How Music Helps in Reducing Stress

It has long been suggested that music can help reduce or manage stress. Consider the trend centered on meditative music created to soothe the mind and inducing relaxation. Fortunately, this is one trend supported by research. Listening to music can be an effective way to cope with stress. In one 2013 study, participants took part in one of three conditions before being exposed to a stressor and then taking a psychosocial stress test. Some participants listened to relaxing music, others listened to the sound of rippling water, and the rest received no auditory stimulation. The results suggested that listening to music had an impact on the human stress response, particularly the autonomic nervous system. Those who had listened to music tended to recover more quickly following a stressor.

How Music Helps You in Sleeping Better

Insomnia is a serious problem that affects people of all age groups. While there are many approaches to treating this problem, research has demonstrated that listening to relaxing classical music can be a safe, effective, and affordable remedy.​ In a study looking at college students, participants listened to classical music, an audiobook, or nothing at all at bedtime for three weeks. Researchers assessed sleep quality both before and after the intervention. The study found that participants who had listened to music had significantly better sleep quality than those who had listened to the audiobook or received no intervention.

Music and Meditations

Combining music with meditation can deepen the positive effects of both, and bring you greater stress relief. As an added bonus, for many people who are beginners to meditation, or who are perfectionists, music meditation can feel simpler and more instantly relaxing than other forms of practice.

TAKE A BREAK



In this modern rat race, we all are moving too fast. All we want to do is achieve success as soon as possible and so we think that taking a break, might be wasting our time. But we are wrong!! We fail to understand that if we go on like this, ignoring our health and just focusing on ‘our definition of success’, which is ‘Money’, we might end up as a mess. Health effects our productivity too, so it becomes even more important to focus on it. We are the hares in the story of ‘The Hare and the Tortoise’ and we need to take that much needed break to relax ourselves. When you take a break, you’re not shirking responsibility. You’re taking care of yourself so you’ll have the stamina to be at your best. Take a look at these signs that might be indicating that you need to pause, so that you can schedule some time away that will help you feel more refreshed and restored.

SIGNS THAT YOU NEED A BREAK

  • Changes in your eating habits
  • Cynicism about your work
  • Difficulty in concentrating
  • Falling ill frequently
  • Lack of energy and motivation
  • Lack of sleep
  • Low mood
  • Frustration.
  • Feeling unfocused or frizzy-headed
  •  Physical symptoms, such as headaches or stomach aches
  • Poor performances at work
  • Using drugs, alcohol or smoking to cope with the stress
  • withdrawing from friends, family or co-workers

HOW TO TAKE A BREAK

We think that break is a flexible term. The idea of a ‘Break’ is different from everybody. For some it is listening to good music might be therapeutic, while for others even drawing can do wonders It's just about finding what suits you, you can either take up a hobby or restart something you enjoyed, but just try doing something different from your daily routine by adding something to it. Now, we will discuss some of the most common ways of taking a break in brief that might work for you too.

VACATION:

The key to a restful vacation is to prioritize rest and fun when you go; don’t overbook yourself with tourist activities or bring so much work with you that by the time you return, you feel you need a vacation from your vacation.

STAYCATION:

It is all about rest and relaxation, and enjoying home sweet home – a place you are often too stressed and busy to really enjoy. The key to a refreshing staycation is, don’t overdo it and don’t let work creep in. You can also turn off your phones, ignore emails and make it a point to bath rest and play at home. “It’s important to still put your OUT OF OFFICE up on your email and try to resist checking your email regularly”, says Dr. Goldman.

SHORT BREAKS:

Sometimes you just need 10 minutes away from your screen to relax your eyes. You can go outdoors for on some time to get some fresh-air, listen to music, watch a movie, do meditation and the list is never ending. You didn’t necessarily need to take leave to take a break, a short-break might be enough for you!
